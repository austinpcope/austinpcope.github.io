About me

I'm a freelance writer, journalist, and audio producer currently based in New York City. My stories span the intersections between politics, the environment, infrastructure, media, and history.

I'm from rural southwestern Colorado and have lived, worked, and studied in Belgium, Ohio, Spain, Wyoming, and northern India. I got my start in professional journalism as a community radio host and reporter at KSJD Radio in my hometown of Cortez. Since then, I've produced hundreds of local interviews, dozens of spot and feature stories, and several long-form articles and audio productions. You can find a full list of my work for KSJD here and my full portfolio below.

In addition to English, I speak French, Spanish, and Hindi. Between 2018 and 2020, I was an Oberlin Shansi Memorial Association fellow, where I worked with Jagori Rural Charitable Trust, a grassroots organization in Himachal Pradesh, India promoting sustainable agriculture and gender equity. I have a degree in Politics from Oberlin College in Ohio, and I am currently studying international reporting at the Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism at the City University of New York.

The Facebook Post That Led to a Recall Election

Washington Post Magazine, "Lost Local News" Special Issue

November 30, 2021

It took only a few minutes for Montezuma-Cortez school board member Lance McDaniel to type, “I’m Antifa,” select a background with orange flames and post to Facebook from his iPhone in early June 2020. It was about a week after George Floyd’s murder, and he was upset.

“I was basically just saying I was anti-fascist,” the 65-year-old McDaniel told me the following summer at a Mexican restaurant in Cortez, Colo., the city with a population of about 8,700 where we both grew up.
